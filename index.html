Good for You

Atlanta Harvest will be the city’s leading source of 100% organic, ultra-local greens and lettuce. Due to Atlanta Harvest’s incredible proximity to market, our produces will often go from farm to table within the same day. This provides our customers with the freshest vegetables possible, at prices competitive with industrial farms. Our vegetables are grown in Certified Organic compost, using only natural fertilizers and nutrients, ensuring our customers receive the most natural, and freshest, produce available.

Good for Farmers

Our company will act as an integrator for aspiring local entrepreneurs, connecting them with the knowledge, resources, and markets that they will need to thrive in Atlanta’s blossoming local food market.

Front-End Services

Atlanta Harvest will specialize in planting new urban farms, by helping new farmers construct, fund, and operate their own independent farm. We will provide all new farmers with training in farming techniques, as well as business skills development, to ensure they have all the proper tools to operate a thriving small business.

Back-End Services

Once started, our company will provide support and distribution services to any of the farms within the Atlanta Harvest network. In addition to a unified brand identity and pooled resources to secure large contracts, Atlanta Harvest farmers gain access to services such as supply chain management, soil testing, and farming equipment.

Great for Atlanta

Atlanta Harvest is a triple-bottom-line company with a big vision: to see urban agriculture form the back bone of thriving neighborhood economies in Atlanta, and across the United States.

Socially, we aim to bring green jobs into a city facing high unemployment and chronically low wages. AH farmers enjoy the benefits of low up-front investment, a true living wage, healthcare benefits, and a guaranteed buyer for their product.

Environmentally, our company reduces Atlanta’s carbon footprint by preserving its green space on the lots we use for farming. We also cut out the thousands of metric tons of CO2 currently created by transporting most of Atlanta’s lettuce and greens across the country from California.
